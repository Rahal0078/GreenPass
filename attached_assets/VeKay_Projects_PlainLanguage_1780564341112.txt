VEKAY SOLAR — PROJECTS MODULE
A Complete Plain-Language Explanation
=====================================

This document explains, in simple words, exactly what the new "Projects"
section is, how it should sit inside your website's admin panel, how it
connects to your Google Sheet, and how it should behave. There are no codes
or scripts here — just a full description of how everything works and looks,
so anyone reading it understands the whole setup.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHAT THIS IS
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Right now your admin panel already has a "Complaints" section where you can
see every customer complaint, open it, and manage it. We are adding a brand
new section next to it called "Projects".

The Projects section is for tracking your solar installation jobs from start
to finish. Each project is one customer's installation, and it moves through
eleven steps — from the day materials are delivered, all the way to the day
the warranty is handed over.

It looks and feels exactly like the Complaints section (same menu style, same
table layout, same colours and buttons), but it works differently inside.
Complaints track problems that need fixing. Projects track an installation
moving forward through its stages.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. WHERE IT SITS IN THE SYSTEM
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In the left-hand menu of the admin panel, there is currently:
Dashboard, Complaints, Customers, Technicians, Reports.

We add one new item — "Projects" — placed right after Complaints. It has a
small sun icon so it stands out as the solar installation tracker. When you
click it, the Projects page opens in the main area, just like clicking
Complaints opens the complaints page.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE CONNECTION TO YOUR GOOGLE SHEET (THE MOST IMPORTANT PART)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Your project data lives in a Google Sheet in your Drive. We connect that
sheet to the system so the two always show the same information.

Here is how the connection behaves:

- The system is connected to ONE specific tab inside your Google Spreadsheet.
  We name that tab "Projects". (Your complaints already use a different tab,
  so they stay separate and don't interfere with each other.)

- There is NO "sync" button anywhere. You should never have to press anything
  to update the sheet. The moment you change anything in the admin panel —
  the second you save it — it is written into the Google Sheet automatically.
  If you change a stage to "Done" at 2:32 in the afternoon, that change is in
  your sheet within a moment, already stamped with the time and date.

- Everything that happens is saved continuously, time after time, with a
  timestamp and date. Nothing is ever lost or left un-saved. Whenever someone
  updates a project, that update appears in the sheet immediately.

- The sheet is the permanent record. So even if you open the Google Sheet
  directly on your phone, you will see the latest version of everything,
  always up to date with what's in the admin panel.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE COLUMNS IN THE GOOGLE SHEET
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The sheet is laid out cleanly, exactly the same neat style as your Complaints
sheet — a bold coloured header row at the top, frozen so it stays visible
when you scroll, and the first few identifying columns frozen so you always
know which customer each row belongs to.

The columns, left to right, are:

  Customer identification (the first part — who the project belongs to):
   1.  SL No                — the serial number of the project
   2.  FL No                — the file number
   3.  Customer Name        — the customer's full name
   4.  Place                — their location / area
   5.  Capacity (KW)        — the system size, e.g. "5 KW DCR", "3 KW"
   6.  Phone                — contact number
   7.  Consumer No (KSEB)   — their KSEB consumer number
   8.  Total Amount         — the total project value in rupees

  The eleven installation stages (the project's progress through the flow):
   9.  Stage 1  — Material Delivery
   10. Stage 2  — Advance 40%
   11. Stage 3  — Welding
   12. Stage 4  — Feasibility
   13. Stage 5  — Wiring
   14. Stage 6  — Commissioning
   15. Stage 7  — Paper Submission
   16. Stage 8  — 2nd Payment 50%
   17. Stage 9  — Deposit Paid
   18. Stage 10 — Account Clear 10%
   19. Stage 11 — Warranty Given

  The record-keeping columns (the history):
   20. Activity Log         — the full timestamped history of every change
   21. Last Updated         — when the row was last touched, in IST
   22. Registered On        — when the project was first created, in IST

The stage columns are colour-coded automatically inside the sheet so you can
read the whole picture at a glance: green when a stage is done, soft yellow
when it's pending, light purple when it's a loan, and light blue when payment
is received. Empty stages stay blank.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
5. WHAT THE PROJECTS PAGE LOOKS LIKE (THE LIST)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When you open Projects, you see a clean table — the same look as the
Complaints list. Each row is one project. Reading across a row you see:

  - FL number
  - Customer name
  - Place
  - Capacity (a small badge showing the KW)
  - A progress bar with a percentage, so you instantly know how far along
    the project is (for example 60% means roughly six or seven stages done)
  - Then eleven small status markers, one for each stage

Each of the eleven stage markers is a small coloured dot or tick:
  - A green tick means that stage is finished
  - A purple marker means a payment-type entry (loan, cash, received)
  - A blue marker means it's actively in progress (a technician is on it)
  - A yellow hourglass means pending
  - A faint dash means not started yet

If you hover your mouse over any stage marker, a little tooltip pops up
showing the actual value — for example the date it was done, or "PENDING",
or the technician's name.

At the top of the page there are filters, just like complaints has filters:
  - A progress filter: show all, or only nearly-complete projects, or only
    in-progress ones, or only new/just-started ones
  - A capacity filter: show all, or only 3 KW, or only 5 KW, or only the
    large systems (8 KW and above)

There is also a "New Project" button in the top right corner.

The list is sorted sensibly — projects that are nearly complete, in progress,
and brand new are grouped so the ones needing attention are easy to find.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. OPENING A PROJECT (THE DETAIL AND EDIT VIEW)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When you click any row, a panel slides in from the right side of the screen.
This is where you see everything about that one project and where you make
all your edits. It has four parts, from top to bottom:

  PART A — Customer Details
  -------------------------
  All the customer's information laid out in neat boxes you can edit directly:
  full name, place, phone, consumer number, capacity, FL number, SL number,
  and total amount. Just type into any box to change it.

  PART B — Installation Flow (the eleven stages)
  ----------------------------------------------
  This is the heart of it. All eleven stages are listed one under the other,
  each numbered 1 to 11, each with its own little coloured dot showing its
  status, its name, and its icon. For each stage you can:

    - Pick a status from a simple dropdown: Not started, Done, Pending,
      Received, Loan, Cash, or Submitted
    - Or type a custom value or a date directly (for example "29/May/26"
      or a technician's name)
    - And below each stage there is a small remark box where you can add a
      short note specific to that stage — for example "KSEB connection
      pending" or "transformer upgrade needed"

  The whole stage row changes colour to match its status, so the panel itself
  is easy to read at a glance.

  PART C — General Remark / Follow-up Note
  ----------------------------------------
  A larger note box for any general comment about the project that isn't tied
  to one particular stage. Whatever you write here gets saved into the history
  with your name and the time.

  PART D — Activity Log (the history)
  -----------------------------------
  At the bottom is a collapsible section showing the complete history of the
  project. It stays small and tidy when closed, but when you open it you see
  every single change ever made, newest at the top, each line reading
  something like:

      28 May 26, 2:32 PM · Admin — Material Delivery: "" → "DONE"
      28 May 26, 2:32 PM · Admin — Note: Transformer upgrade needed
      29 May 26, 10:00 AM · Admin — Advance 40%: → LOAN

  This log is never edited by hand — the system writes it for you. It is your
  permanent, trustworthy record of who did what and when.

  Saving:
  When you've made your changes, you press "Save & Sync". The changes are
  saved, the history is updated with a timestamp, and the Google Sheet is
  updated instantly — all in one action. A small confirmation appears. There
  is a gentle reminder at the bottom that every save goes straight to your
  Google Sheet with no extra step.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
7. ADDING A NEW PROJECT
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Pressing the "New Project" button opens a small form. You fill in:

  - Customer name (required)
  - Place (required)
  - Capacity in KW (required — pick from a list, or choose "custom" to type
    something like "5 with 3 KW hybrid")
  - FL number
  - SL number
  - Phone
  - KSEB consumer number
  - Total amount in rupees
  - An optional first remark

When you press "Create Project":
  - The project is created
  - It automatically gets the next serial number
  - The history records "Project created" with the date, time, and your name
  - A new row appears in the Google Sheet straight away
  - The new project's detail panel opens so you can start filling in its stages

The eleven stages all start empty (not started), and you fill them in over
time as the installation progresses.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8. EDITING AND FOLLOW-UP — HOW THE FLOW WORKS DAY TO DAY
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The whole point of this section is to make following up on each job simple and
professional. Here's the everyday rhythm:

  - A new enquiry comes in → you add a new project. It shows as "new" in the
    list with an empty progress bar.

  - Materials get delivered → you open the project, set Stage 1 to Done. The
    progress bar moves up, the sheet updates, the history records it.

  - Advance is collected → set Stage 2 to the payment type (Loan / Cash /
    Received). Add a remark if needed, like "1 lakh paid, balance on loan".

  - And so on through welding, feasibility, wiring, commissioning, paperwork,
    second payment, deposit, account clearing, and finally warranty.

  - At every step you can leave a remark, so the next person who opens it
    knows exactly what's happening and what to follow up on.

  - At any time you can glance at the list and instantly see which projects
    are stuck, which are nearly done, and which haven't started — and act on
    them.

Every edit, every remark, every status change is saved the moment you make it,
stamped with the date and time, visible both in the admin panel and in your
Google Sheet.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
9. WHAT NEEDS TO HAPPEN TO SET IT UP (IN PLAIN WORDS)
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For your developer, the setup is small and clean. In plain language, these are
the things that need doing — no need to rebuild anything that already exists:

  1. Add a new storage table in the database to hold projects (a one-time
     setup that the system can create automatically).

  2. Add the new Projects page into the admin panel — the page already
     follows the same design as the Complaints page, so it fits right in.

  3. Add the "Projects" item to the left-hand menu, between Complaints and
     Customers, with the sun icon.

  4. Connect it to the Google Sheet — point it at a new tab named "Projects"
     inside the spreadsheet that's already connected to the system, and share
     that sheet with the same service account already in use. No new accounts
     or new logins are needed.

  5. That's it. Once those are in place, adding, editing, and following up on
     projects all work, and everything writes to the Google Sheet instantly.


-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10. THE SHORT SUMMARY
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - A new "Projects" section in the admin panel, sitting beside Complaints.
  - It tracks each solar installation through 11 clear stages.
  - It looks exactly like the Complaints section but works as a forward-moving
    installation tracker instead of a complaint handler.
  - It is connected live to a "Projects" tab in your Google Sheet.
  - There is no sync button — every change saves and updates the sheet
    instantly, with the date and time recorded.
  - Every project keeps a full, timestamped history of who changed what and
    when, kept small and tidy but fully viewable when opened.
  - Every stage and the project overall can carry remarks, so follow-up is
    easy and professional.
  - You can add new projects, edit any detail, filter the list, and see each
    project's progress at a glance.

The result is one clean, professional place to run your whole installation
pipeline — easy to handle, easy to understand, and always in sync with your
Google Sheet.
